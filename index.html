Piano History

The piano was invented in the early 18th century by Italian musician Bartolomeo Cristofori. Pianos were originally made by hand and most high quality pianos are still made by hand. Many different types of pianos have been
created over the years, from portable keyboards, to grand pianos, to electric pianos. The new improvements gradually evolved the piano into an instrument for more solo performances and at-home use. The original name for the piano was the piano et forte, meaning soft and loud. This refers to the large range of notes that can be played on the
piano, from low bass clef notes to high treble clef notes. It is one of the few instruments that can produce such a versatile range of tones.

Inner Workings

When a pianist presses a black or white key on the piano, it causes a hammer inside the piano to lift and
strike down on the string that is attached to the specific note being played. The strings vibrate which
makes a noise that is then amplified by the soundboard. When the key is released, the string stops vibrating
and the noise stops. The original name for the piano was the piano et forte, meaning soft and loud. This refers to the large range of notes that can be played on the piano, from low bass clef notes to high treble clef notes. It is one of the
few instruments that can produce such a versatile range of tones.

Piano Guilds

The Piano Guild is a division of the American College of Musicians founded in 1929 by Dr. Irl Allison. The primary function of the Piano Guild is to establish definite goals and awards in competitive auditions to students of all ages
and skill sets. Students perform several pieces of music from different musical eras and perform them for a group of judges. Students are judged based on accuracy, continuity, phrasing, pedaling, dynamics, rhythm, tempo, tone,
interpretation, style, and technique. There is also normally a test on music theory. Students can compete at a local level, district level, state level, national level, and international level. Students receive report cards with constructive
criticism, as well as certificates, medals, trophies, and pins depending on what level they compete at.

Personal History

I started taking piano lessons when I was 6 years old, and started doing guild when I was 10 years old. I am going into my 12th year of taking lessons and 8th of guild. I have taken with 4 different teachers as of right now. For the past 6 years, I have taken lessons at Lawson Academy of the Arts and Converse College. Before then, I took lessons at Inman Recreation Center. My favorite era of piano composition is the Baroque era, which spanned from 1600-1750. My favorite composers from that era are George Handel and Antonio Vivaldi.
